About Me

I'm Ananya Gutgutia, a nineteen-year-old visual communication student from Chennai, currently studying in Mumbai. I see design as a way of observing, feeling, and translating the world around me. My work often explores themes of simplicity, sensitivity, and the quiet beauty tucked into everyday moments.

I enjoy moving fluidly between mediums—digital, print, and tactile—each one giving me a different language to express ideas that feel both thoughtful and instinctive. I'm drawn to creating work that carries a sense of calmness, yet still leaves room for curiosity, play, and discovery.

At its core, my practice is about paying attention: to details, emotions, and the subtle interactions that shape how we experience the world. Design, for me, is a way of making those moments visible.

Project 1: Stippling & Emotion

This piece grew out of my interest in how small, repetitive marks can slowly build form and emotion. Working only with stippling, I spent a long time layering dots to shape the quiet contours of the feet, letting the drawing develop at its own pace.

The dripping ink wasn't part of my original plan, but it naturally emerged as I worked and began to feel like a meaningful element. For me, it suggests a mix of release and weight - something slowly slipping away yet still present.

Project 2: Try Again

This piece grew from my interest in how repetition can become both a visual language and an emotional one. I used a grid-based system and slowly increased the density of the word 'TRY' to create a sense of depth and momentum, almost like the viewer is being pulled forward.

The distortion and layering were intentional. It's built from many attempts, stacked again and again, until they finally form something larger, conveying a message of perseverance.

Project 3: Vision & Perception

I wanted to explore the idea of vision not just as sight, but as perception—the way we interpret, distort, and expand what we see. The floating eyeball became a symbol of that: detached, exposed, and hyper-aware.

I focused on creating a glossy, almost sci-fi texture to the eyeball, with exaggerated veins and color gradients. The cool purples and electric greens were chosen to give the piece a digital mood, contrasting with the organic form. For me, this artwork reflects my interest in blending realism with digital aesthetics.
